A job search bar for job findings according to one's preference. It takes 4 inputs from users to show relevant results. Only 60% users who visited the
website actually used this search bar which is considered to be low by industry standards.
Takings 4 inputs in the landing page itself confuses the users to a certain extent. Consequently, the inputs were reduced. This helped the user to get
more focused on what he wants i.e job profile he's looking to work in.

Primary concerns are profile and job locations. Experience and salary expectations are a secondary concerns according to user research and
competitive analysis from other similar platforms. Hence, the other two eliminated. The entire search bar was redesigned. Letters, icons and lines all
have the same width i.e 1px to give a visually uniform appearance. Hint texts are now used.
Two options were available to apply for any job.

1. Register and apply.
2. Apply without register.

Based on analytics software integrated in the platform, 74% users were opting for the second option. From business perspectives, it was essential that
they make an account with us.
The journey of applying without register had to be designed in such a way that they are triggered about it effortlessly. Also, the entire process is
supposed to be as much user-centric as possible. With few clicks, the user should be able to apply for the job and in the process, make an acoount.
The need for this was triggered by the fact that they could track their applications which they applied just then.
